4. Super Mario 64


Released in 1996 for the Nintendo 64, Super Mario 64 is a groundbreaking title that redefined the platforming genre and set a new standard for 3D video games. As Mario's first foray into the third dimension, this iconic game introduced a revolutionary approach to level design and gameplay mechanics, earning it a permanent place in the annals of gaming history. Its influence extends far beyond its original release, impacting countless subsequent titles and cementing its status as one of the greatest games of all time.


Super Mario 64 transports players into a vibrant, 3D rendition of the Mushroom Kingdom, where they control Mario on a quest to rescue Princess Peach from Bowser. Unlike the 2D side-scrolling Mario games that preceded it, Super Mario 64 offers a fully three-dimensional world, allowing players to explore expansive environments from multiple angles and perspectives. The game is set within Princess Peach's castle, which serves as a central hub. From here, players can access various paintings that act as portals to different worlds, each with its own unique theme and challenges. These worlds are populated with a variety of enemies, collectibles, and intricate platforming sections. One of the game’s most notable features is its open-ended level design. Each world contains multiple objectives and stars to collect, encouraging exploration and experimentation. The freedom to approach tasks in different ways adds a layer of depth and replayability. The game's physics and controls also played a significant role in its success, with Mario’s movements feeling fluid and responsive, making the platforming experience both intuitive and engaging. Super Mario 64 also introduced several new gameplay mechanics that have since become staples of the franchise, including Mario’s ability to perform triple jumps, wall kicks, and dives. These mechanics not only enhanced the platforming experience but also allowed for a greater degree of creativity and skill expression.


Why it's one of the best:

  1. Revolutionary 3D Gameplay: Super Mario 64 was one of the first games to successfully transition a beloved 2D franchise into a 3D environment. Its innovative approach to 3D level design and control mechanics set a new standard for future games in the genre, influencing how 3D platformers were developed.
  2. Open-Ended Exploration: The game’s non-linear level structure allows for a high degree of freedom and exploration. Players can choose how to approach challenges and objectives, which adds a sense of autonomy and replayability.
  3. Influential Design: Super Mario 64 introduced a number of gameplay mechanics and design principles that have become fundamental in the gaming industry. Its impact on 3D game design is still felt today, as many modern games build upon the foundation it established.
  4. Timeless Appeal: The game’s engaging gameplay, charming visuals, and memorable soundtrack have ensured its enduring popularity. It remains a beloved classic, frequently cited in discussions about the greatest games ever made.
  5. Critical Acclaim and Legacy: Super Mario 64 received widespread acclaim upon release and has continued to be celebrated for its innovation and quality. It has been included in numerous "best of all time" lists and is regarded as a seminal title in the history of video games.

In summary, Super Mario 64 is celebrated for its revolutionary approach to 3D platforming, its open-ended and exploratory gameplay, and its lasting influence on the gaming industry. Its pioneering design and timeless appeal make it a standout title in the pantheon of great video games.
