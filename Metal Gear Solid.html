10. Metal Gear Solid



And last on the list, Released in 1998 by Konami for the PlayStation, Metal Gear Solid is a landmark title in the stealth action genre and a seminal work in the career of its creator, Hideo Kojima. Renowned for its innovative gameplay mechanics, cinematic presentation, and deep narrative, Metal Gear Solid set new standards for video game storytelling and design. Its influence is still felt across the gaming industry, making it a pivotal and iconic title.


Metal Gear Solid follows the story of Solid Snake, a skilled operative who is summoned to thwart a nuclear threat posed by a rogue special forces unit known as FOXHOUND. The unit has seized control of a nuclear weapon-equipped facility on Shadow Moses Island, and it is up to Snake to infiltrate the facility, uncover the truth behind the threat, and stop the potential disaster. The gameplay of Metal Gear Solid is centered around stealth, strategy, and tactical thinking. Unlike traditional action games, players are encouraged to avoid direct confrontation whenever possible. The game’s stealth mechanics involve using the environment to hide from enemies, employing various gadgets and tools, and carefully planning movements to avoid detection. This approach creates a tension-filled experience that rewards careful planning and execution. One of the game’s most notable features is its cinematic presentation. Metal Gear Solid incorporates a complex narrative with extensive dialogue, cutscenes, and character development. The story is rich with political intrigue, personal drama, and philosophical themes, adding depth to the gameplay experience. The game’s cinematic style was groundbreaking at the time, blurring the lines between video games and film. The game also introduced several memorable characters, such as the enigmatic antagonist Liquid Snake and the formidable Meryl Silverburgh. These characters, along with the game’s intricate plot and dramatic twists, contribute to the overall depth and engagement of the story. The technical aspects of Metal Gear Solid were also impressive for its time. The game featured detailed graphics, advanced AI for enemy behavior, and a highly atmospheric soundtrack composed by Tappi Iwase. These elements combined to create an immersive and engaging experience.

Why it's one of the best:


  1. Innovative Stealth Mechanics: Metal Gear Solid popularized the stealth genre, introducing gameplay mechanics that focused on evasion and strategy rather than direct combat. This approach provided a fresh and engaging experience that has influenced many subsequent titles.
  2. Cinematic Storytelling: The game’s integration of extensive cutscenes, dialogue, and cinematic presentation set new standards for storytelling in video games. The narrative depth and character development were groundbreaking and have been highly influential in the industry.
  3. Memorable Characters and Plot: The complex and multifaceted characters, along with the gripping and dramatic plot, make Metal Gear Solid a standout in terms of narrative. The game’s story is filled with memorable moments and unexpected twists.
  4. Technical Achievement: For its time, Metal Gear Solid was a technical marvel, with detailed graphics, sophisticated enemy AI, and a high-quality soundtrack. These elements contributed to the game’s immersive experience and overall quality.
  5. Critical Acclaim and Legacy: The game received widespread critical acclaim for its innovation, storytelling, and technical achievements. It has been recognized as one of the greatest games of all time and has had a lasting impact on the gaming industry.
